Psychological counselling

Have you ever found yourself in a challenging or painful situation? Or have you ever felt the need for a change but not been able to identify the direction in which you should go? Have you ever wondered: What do I really want? How am I really?

The questions I could add to this list are many, and certainly all of us, sooner or later, find ourselves asking them. Sometimes to ourselves, sometimes with the people closest to us, and sometimes we may feel the need or desire to share these questions or these more complex pieces of our path with a psychologist, a person outside our environment, who can listen to us without prejudice and without judging us.
A free, open and safe space.

Here, this is what I do in my job: accompany you for a piece of that path on which you are walking and help you to continue trying to understand what is happening inside and outside.

Talking to a mental health professional

Why ask for help, and how?

We often have a static idea of ourselves and what is around us (people, environment...) and this makes it difficult to create space for a change, for a new perspective that can unblock us or help us get out of a situation that no longer suits us.

It is not easy to ask a mental health professional for help for several reasons.
First, we need to become aware that a part of us is suffering or needs more care and attention. Suffering, the desire for confrontation, the desire to know ourselves more deeply are all part of our being human, recognising and entering into these dimensions means also taking care of ourselves.

About me

About me

I am a psychologist and trainee psychotherapist at the Institute of Constructivist Psychology di Padova. After a little over a decade living outside my hometown, I returned to Trento, where I work as psychologist, offering individual and couples counselling, both online and in person.

Before returning to my beloved mountains, I worked as a psychologist for a few years at the Vrije Universiteit Brussel (VUB), offering psychological support to students and researchers. It was definitely an enriching experience: dealing with the international community, I had the opportunity to meet and offer psychological counselling to people from all over the world. In addition to providing individual support, I was also a trainer for several group trainings, including trainings based on Mindfulness and Self-Compassion and ACT (Acceptance and Commitment Therapy), as well as trainings about coping with fear of failure and blocking situations.

Before moving to Belgium, I collaborated with some cooperatives in Trentino, where I conducted sexuality and affectivity education for children and adolescents. During my postgraduate training, I trained at the Adoption Centre in Padua (ULSS6) and later in Vienna, at the psychotherapy association Ediveria.

I currently collaborate with associated psychologist practices in Trento and online with CATP (Centre d'Accompagnement Thérapeutique Pluridisciplinaire - Bruxelles).

If you wish to know more about my education, you can check my LinkedIn profile .
